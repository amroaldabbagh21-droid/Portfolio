Cybersecurity Learning Path

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, or damage. In today's digital world, cybersecurity is essential because almost everything we do depends on technology.

Protecting Data

Learn to keep sensitive information safe from hackers and unauthorized access.

Understanding Threats

Master common threats like viruses, malware, phishing, and ransomware attacks.

Network Security

Secure Wi-Fi networks and company systems to prevent unauthorized access.

Ethical Hacking

Test systems for weaknesses to help organizations strengthen their security.

Types of Hackers

Understanding the different motivations and methodologies in the cybersecurity landscape

White Hat Hackers

The "good" hackers who use their knowledge to help companies find and fix security problems. They work as ethical hackers with proper authorization.

Black Hat Hackers

Cybercriminals who exploit vulnerabilities for personal gain, data theft, extortion, or spreading malware with malicious intent.

Script Kiddies

Inexperienced hackers who use pre-made tools and scripts without understanding how they work, often causing random damage.

Security Researchers

Experts who discover new vulnerabilities and develop exploits, often working with companies to improve security.

Metasploit Framework

The most widely used penetration testing framework that helps security professionals identify and exploit vulnerabilities.

Exploits

Pre-written code to exploit known vulnerabilities in systems

Payloads

Execute actions on target systems after successful exploitation

Auxiliary Modules

Network scanning, traffic sniffing, and password brute-forcing

Post-Exploitation

Further exploration and privilege escalation after access

Our Mission: Find the Flaws, Fix the Future!

Step 1: The Digital Treasure Hunt

We're a crew of curious minds who call ourselves TypeHackers. Our first mission is a giant treasure hunt for digital bugs! We scan, poke, and prod at software, looking for that one loose brick in the digital wall. A tiny mistake in the code? That's our treasure map!

Step 2: Unleashing Metasploit

When we find a vulnerability, we deploy our favorite digital dragon: Metasploit! Think of it as our ultimate utility belt packed with pre-built keys for known locks. The real glory comes when we forge our own keys by writing custom exploits.

Step 3: Post-Exploitation Analysis

Getting inside is just the beginning. We explore systems, analyze access levels, and understand how far a tiny flaw can propagate. This "post-exploitation party" helps us understand the real impact of vulnerabilities.
